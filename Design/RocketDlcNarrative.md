# Rocket DLC Narrative — "Starboard Launch"

> **Source of truth:** `Design/NarrativeStructure.md` (base narrative) · `Design/IslandProgression.md` (island sequence) · `Design/ConceptCurriculum.md` (concept list)  
> **Status:** Canonical DLC story arc, cinematic beat map, and narrative-gameplay alignment.

---

## Thesis

> *"The same pirate-cartographer who charted the Memory Sea now charts the stars — because knowledge doesn't stop at the horizon."*

After completing the Golden Chart, Nemo discovers a weathered star chart pinned beneath the navigation desk. The chart maps a chain of sky-facing islands whose lighthouses once aimed upward. The rocket science DLC extends the place-and-recall loop into orbital mechanics, thrust, and re-entry — the same structured-memory skills, but with increasingly abstract concepts. The Memory Sea's lesson applies everywhere, even in space.

---

## Core Fantasy

Nemo is no longer just a pirate. She is a **pirate-astronaut** — someone who understands that the same spatial memory method that conquered the sea can conquer the void above it. The star chart is older and more fragmentary than the Golden Chart. It was abandoned by the original cartographers when they fled the Space Kraken, a cosmic cousin of the deep-sea Kraken that guards the edge of known sky.

Bit adapts immediately (Bit always adapts). The ship *Loci* gains makeshift rocket boosters bolted to its hull. The sea becomes the launchpad. The sky becomes the new frontier.

## Three-Act Structure

### Act 1 — Building the Ship (Stages 1–2)

**Theme:** Preparation. You can't reach the stars without foundations.

| Beat | Narrative | Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1 Intro** | The *Loci* arrives at Launchpad Lagoon, a sun-kissed cove with an ancient stone rocket protruding from the beach. Nemo spots it at dawn. Bit buzzes with excitement. The star chart's first fragment glows faintly. | Cinematic → encode 3 concepts (thrust, propulsion, trajectory) |
| **Stage 1 Outro** | Nemo earns the first star chart fragment. She and Bit test-fire a small rocket from the lagoon shore — it arcs, trails exhaust, and crashes into the surf. Progress, not perfection. | Star chart reward + teaser for Booster Reef |
| **Sailing 1→2** | Storm clouds roll in. Lightning arcs across the reef ahead. This will not be easy. | Overworld transition |
| **Stage 2 Intro** | Booster Reef — a jagged reef constantly struck by lightning. Ancient booster hardware is bolted to the rocks, corroded but recognizable. The storm howls. Bit shivers. Nemo braces. | Cinematic → encode 3 concepts (escape velocity, fuel efficiency, staging) |
| **Stage 2 Outro** | The rocket fires upward and punches through clouds. The exhaust plume fills the sky. A new star chart fragment locks into place. For the first time, the *Loci*'s bow angles skyward. Beyond the atmosphere — stars. | Chart fragment + rocket ignition FX |

**Emotional arc:** Wonder → determination → first breakthrough (leaving the atmosphere).

**Narrative delivery:** Environmental: the rocket on the beach tells the story. No dialogue. The weather teaches challenge. The exhaust plume is the reward visual.

---

### Act 2 — Voyage to the Stars (Stages 3–4)

**Theme:** Awe and growing danger. Space is beautiful and lethal.

| Beat | Narrative | Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 3 Intro** | Orbit Atoll — an impossible floating ring of coral and machinery, glowing against the night sky. Orbit rings encircle it. Asteroids drift lazily. Nemo gasps. Bit hums. | Cinematic → encode 3 concepts (orbital mechanics, delta-V, gravity assists) |
| **Stage 3 Outro** | Star chart fragment earned. But as Nemo turns to leave, a single massive tentacle drifts past the viewport. The screen shakes. Bit freezes. Something is watching from the dark. | Stinger: tentacle + shake |
| **Sailing 3→4** | The void deepens. Stars thin. A nebula glows ahead — beautiful, but the light hides something. | Overworld transition into dark_sea |
| **Stage 4 Intro** | Nebula Shallows — a translucent cloud of gas and dust, lit from within by dying stars. Void eels flicker at the edges. The ship creaks. Even Bit goes quiet. | Cinematic → encode 3 concepts (heat shields, re-entry dynamics, atmospheric drag) |
| **Stage 4 Outro** | Star chart fragment earned. But tentacles — plural now — slide across the hull. A deep rumble. Nemo steadies the wheel. "The Kraken of the Sky." Bit chatters nervously. The chart shows one final island ahead, shrouded in absolute darkness. | Foreshadowing: multiple tentacles + shake escalation |

**Emotional arc:** Awe → unease → dread. The space is gorgeous but something stalks you.

**Narrative delivery:**
- Orbit Atoll: visual wonder (rings, asteroids, night sky).
- Nebula Shallows: environmental dread (fog of nebula, void eel flickers).
- Tentacle stingers are the Act 2 recurring foreshadowing motif, escalating from 1 to many.
- No dialogue. No text. But the player *knows* something is coming.

---

### Act 3 — The Kraken's Void (Stage 5)

**Theme:** Cumulative mastery under maximum threat, then triumphant return.

| Beat | Narrative | Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 5 Intro (beat 1)** | The Kraken's Void — absolute darkness. Tentacles drift everywhere. The ship is tiny. A single heartbeat drum begins. | Cinematic (dark_sea, establish dread) |
| **Stage 5 Intro (beat 2)** | Tentacles constrict. The ship groans. Shake intensifies. | Tension escalation |
| **Stage 5 Intro (beat 3)** | The Space Kraken *emerges* — a cosmic horror version of the base-game Kraken, but wreathed in nebula gas and studded with dead stars. Eyes like dying supernovae. Maximum shake. | Boss reveal (space_kraken emerge) |
| **Stage 5 Intro (beat 4)** | Nemo grips the wheel. Bit lands on her shoulder. The star chart fragments glow — they'll need everything they've learned. | Resolve beat (Nemo + Bit together) |
| **GAMEPLAY** | Boss encounter: recall concepts from ALL 5 DLC islands. Each correct recall pushes the Kraken back. Each failure tightens its grip. | 5–8 cross-island recall prompts |
| **Stage 5 Outro (beat 1)** | The Space Kraken shudders, releases the *Loci*, and sinks into the void — not destroyed, but satisfied. The test was passed. | space_kraken sink animation |
| **Stage 5 Outro (beat 2)** | Re-entry begins. Flames lick the hull (reentry_flame). The star chart is complete — it shows the way home. | Re-entry phase 1: fire |
| **Stage 5 Outro (beat 3)** | The sky lightens from dark to dusk. Tinted heat haze. The ship rattles but holds. | Re-entry phase 2: atmosphere |
| **Stage 5 Outro (beat 4)** | Parachutes deploy. Day sky. The sea glitters below. Descent. | Re-entry phase 3: parachute |
| **Stage 5 Outro (beat 5)** | SPLASHDOWN. The *Loci* hits the water in a fountain of spray. Dawn sky. Nemo and Bit celebrate, leaping and buzzing. Complete star chart glows on the deck. | Splashdown triumph |
| **Stage 5 Outro (beat 6)** | Wide shot: the *Loci* sails into the sunrise. All islands — both sea and sky — are charted. A true pirate-scientist. The horizon holds new questions. | Epilogue (dawn + sunrise) |

**Emotional arc:** Dread → escalating intensity → desperate hope → catharsis → pure joy → pride → curiosity.

**Narrative delivery:**
- The Space Kraken is the DLC's final exam, mirroring the base game's Kraken structure.
- The re-entry sequence (5 beats) is the DLC's unique climax: it transforms "winning the boss fight" into a physical journey home. Defeat → fire → atmosphere → parachute → splash. Each beat has escalating sky brightness (dark_sea → night → dusk → day → dawn). The player feels the return to safety.
- Splashdown is the emotional peak: the moment where abstract orbital mechanics knowledge literally brings you safely home.
- The final sunrise shot mirrors the base game's ending — the sea and sky are both charted now.

|---|---|---|---|
| **Nemo** | Discovers the star chart, excited but uncertain about leaving the sea | Gains confidence in space, awed by the cosmos | Faces the Space Kraken, earns the title "Star Cartographer" |

| **Space Kraken** | Absent | Foreshadowed via tentacles (Act 2 stingers) | The cosmic gatekeeper — tests, does not destroy |

## Thematic Throughline

**"Structured memory works everywhere — even in space."**

The base game established that spatial association beats brute memorization. The DLC extends this thesis: if the Method of Loci works for AI fundamentals on a pirate sea, it works for rocket science in the void above. The same player, the same method, a harder domain. The message: **you are not limited to what you've already learned.**

- Nemo (Method of Loci) succeeds again.
- The Space Kraken (cosmic test) is satisfied, not angered — it was waiting for someone worthy.
- The re-entry sequence is the metaphor made literal: **knowledge gets you home safely.**

## Star Chart as Reward Motif

The star chart replaces the base game's chart fragments as the progressive reward:

| After Stage | Star Chart State |
|---|---|
| 1 | 1/5 filled — first constellation appears |
| 2 | 2/5 filled — path through atmosphere visible |
| 3 | 3/5 filled — orbital routes mapped |
| 4 | 4/5 filled — return trajectory plotted |
| 5 | Complete — all routes charted, sea-to-sky-to-sea |

**Rule:** The star_chart prop appears in every island outro, always in a celebratory beat following concept mastery.

## Lore Delivery System (DLC)

The DLC follows the base game's no-text-in-gameplay rule. All narrative is environmental:

| Method | DLC Usage | Required? |
|---|---|---|
| **Environmental storytelling** | Stone rocket, booster hardware, orbit rings, nebula fog, void eels | Yes |
| **Props as narrative** | Rocket → exhaust_plume → asteroids → nebula → reentry_flame → parachute → splash | Yes |
| **Sky mood progression** | Dawn → Storm → Night → Dark Sea → Dark Sea → Dawn | Yes |
| **Tentacle foreshadowing** | Single tentacle (Stage 3 outro) → multiple (Stage 4 outro) → constriction (Stage 5 intro) | Yes |
| **Star chart fragments** | Post-stage reward in every outro | Yes |
| **Re-entry sequence** | 5 visual beats telling the story of coming home | Yes |
| **Optional lore objects** | Ancient cartographer star maps on islands 3–4 (pictographic panels) | No |

**Rule:** If the player skips every optional element, they still understand: *I built a rocket, went to space, fought a cosmic kraken, and came home.*
